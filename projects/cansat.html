Mechanical Engineer — CanSat @ Virginia Tech

  • Designed, manufactured, and tested components using iterative design cycles to optimize performance.
  • Performed fabrication using laser cutting, CNC machining, 3D printing, soldering, crimping, and cable routing.
  • Helped precision-assemble probe modules, ensuring weight, tolerance, and integration requirements were met.

  • Team placed 38th of 100+ teams worldwide at the NASA & Lockheed Martin–sponsored AAS CanSat competition.

Design Overview

The payload was modeled in SolidWorks as a fully modular system, allowing rapid replacement and testing of nosecone, burn-wire deployment, electronics, and carbon‑fiber structural modules. The design emphasized low mass, high strength, and manufacturability.

Key Subsystems

1. Carbon Fiber Nosecone & Structural Modules

Used custom 3D‑printed molds and vacuum bagging to manufacture carbon‑fiber components. This significantly reduced weight while maintaining strength and heat resistance.

2. Burn‑Wire Deployment Mechanism

Designed and integrated a two‑stage MOSFET + capacitor switching system enabling reliable deployment at low voltages (3.3–12V). Tuned for rapid aerobrake release.

3. PCB‑Based Flight Computer

Transitioned from protoboard to a custom 2‑layer PCB, improving vibration resistance and reducing mass. Iterated routing for improved sensor spacing, shorter traces, and better thermal behavior.
