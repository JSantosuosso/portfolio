e‑NABLE Low‑Cost Prosthetic Hand

Role & Objectives

Mechatronics Engineer — e‑NABLE @ Virginia Tech
Aug 2024 – Present

  • Designed a modular, serviceable palm interior for a low‑cost prosthetic hand.
  • Optimized internal component layout to improve stability, tactile feel, and motion smoothness.
  • Developed an improved tendon spool layout to maximize torque transfer with minimal part mass.
  • Presented research at NCUR & NCRC national research conferences.

Internal Palm Architecture

The palm serves as the central structural component of the hand, containing servo mounts, guide channels, tendon routing features, and load‑bearing geometry. I developed a compact layout that reduced empty volume, improved tendon alignment, and increased overall grip rigidity.

  • Servo Layout: Servo mounts were redesigned to reduce torsional looseness and resist cyclic loading.
  • Tendon Routing: Integrated channels ensure constrained cable paths with repeatable tensioning.
  • Load Path: Rib structures mimic metacarpal stiffness, distributing grasp forces across the palm.

Servo & Spool System

The spool assembly transforms rotational servo output into tendon‑driven flexion. I worked on refining the spool width, cable capture grooves, and guide geometry to reduce slipping and improve force transmission.

  • Modified tension grooves to prevent tendon overwrap.
  • Improved clamping system for repeatable cable installation.
  • Balanced spool radius optimizing torque vs. travel.

Outcome & Impact

This design enables a low‑cost, serviceable, customizable prosthetic hand with reliable motion and low material cost. These improvements contribute directly to accessibility, repairability, and realistic grasp quality for low‑resource prosthetic users.
